

## QUESTIONS:

Opening

1. Dr. Kini, when you look at the future of Manipal Academy of Higher Education, what is the next chapter you envision for this institution?

Answer

I am Dr. Kini, and it is my privilege to speak about the next chapter of Manipal Academy of Higher Education.

To understand where we are going, we must first remember where we began.

In 1953, when Manipal Academy of Higher Education was founded, India was on the threshold of independence. Yet the founders of this institution believed deeply that the future of a nation would be shaped through education — through knowledge, innovation, and people prepared to solve real challenges.

In many ways, the journey of Manipal Academy of Higher Education has always grown alongside the journey of India itself. Over the decades, our campus has nurtured doctors, scientists, entrepreneurs, educators, technologists, and leaders who have contributed to society across the world.

Today, we stand at another historic moment.

Our nation has set an ambitious goal — the vision of Viksit Bharat 2047, where India becomes a fully developed nation by 2047.

And that raises an important question for institutions like ours.

What role must universities play in shaping that future?

2. — Industry Insight

When you speak to industry leaders today, what are they telling you about the future of talent and education?

Answer

When I speak with leaders across industries and sectors, they often tell me something very encouraging about our students. They say MAHE students are intelligent, hardworking, and academically strong.

But they also highlight something equally important.

Today’s organizations do not simply hire degrees. They look for clearly demonstrated skills aligned with specific roles.

Traditionally, students graduate with degrees across many disciplines — medicine, engineering, business, design, science, and the humanities. But when they enter organizations, employers still need time to understand the specific strengths and capabilities each graduate brings.

The challenge is not talent.

The challenge is visibility of skills.

And that realization has shaped the next transformation of Manipal Academy of Higher Education.

Our goal is to create an ecosystem where education, skills, and real-world opportunities are clearly connected.

3. The Frameworks

What are the key frameworks MAHE is introducing to bridge this gap between education and the professional world?

Answer

To achieve this, we are introducing two powerful frameworks across the campus.

The first is Skill Taxonomy.

This will map every capability our students can develop — from disciplinary foundations to emerging technologies, creativity, research skills, leadership, analytics, design thinking, and innovation.

Students will no longer experience education as disconnected subjects. Instead, they will see a clear map of the capabilities they are developing throughout their academic journey.

The second framework is Job Taxonomy.

This connects those skills directly to real roles that exist across industries and sectors — roles such as product manager, growth strategist, operations analyst, digital commerce specialist, and cybersecurity analyst.

When students understand how their skills connect to real-world opportunities, something remarkable happens.

Learning gains purpose.

4. — The Future Student

Can you share a story that illustrates the kind of student MAHE hopes to shape in the future?

Answer

Let me share a story that represents the future we envision.

Imagine a student entering Manipal Academy of Higher Education in the second year. Like many students, he initially believes success means studying well, completing assignments, and waiting for placement season.

But when he begins exploring the Skill Taxonomy system, he discovers something new about himself. He realizes he enjoys working with data, digital systems, and solving operational problems.

Through the Job Taxonomy framework, he sees that these interests align with emerging roles in areas such as digital commerce, analytics, and product development.

Suddenly learning becomes more than a syllabus.

Every project, every internship, every competition becomes a step toward building real capability.

By the third year, the student begins working on a project that addresses a real-world challenge — perhaps improving a digital service, analyzing data to solve a business problem, or building a prototype solution for an industry partner.

What begins as a classroom idea becomes a working solution. Faculty guide the exploration. Industry mentors refine the direction.

When the student graduates, he is not simply another graduate searching for a job.

He enters an organization as an intrapreneur — someone capable of creating new ideas and value within the institution.

This is the kind of graduate we want to shape at Manipal Academy of Higher Education.

Students who do not just study subjects.

Students who build meaningful solutions for society.

5. The Ecosystem

Beyond students, what role will faculty, alumni, and industry partners play in this transformation?

Answer

This transformation will not be limited to students alone.

Our faculty will play an even more powerful role in this journey.

Every faculty member at Manipal Academy of Higher Education is not just teaching a subject. They are mentors shaping the next generation of thinkers, innovators, and leaders.

Our industry partners will not simply visit our campus for recruitment. They will collaborate with us to co-create learning experiences and real-world problem solving.

Our alumni will become mentors, guides, and innovators within this ecosystem.

Together, we will transform this campus into a place where skills are visible, innovation is constant, and students graduate ready to create value for society.

6. The 2046 Milestone

Why is the timeline of 2046 so meaningful for MAHE?

Answer

This vision becomes even more meaningful when we look at our timeline.

Manipal Academy of Higher Education was founded in 1953.

In 2046, we will celebrate 100 years of this remarkable institution.

And by that centenary year, we have set ourselves a bold goal.

Before India reaches Viksit Bharat 2047, Manipal Academy of Higher Education will demonstrate what a truly future-ready university ecosystem looks like.

Our centenary vision is simple and powerful:

“MAHE 2046 — Shaping the Future of Education for Viksit Bharat.”

By the time we celebrate our hundredth year, we want the nation to look at MAHE and see proof that this future is not distant.

It is already possible.

Because institutions like Manipal Academy of Higher Education must not simply witness the rise of a developed India.

They must help build it.

By 2046, we will show the world what a century of belief in education can achieve.
